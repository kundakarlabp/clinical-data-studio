# Case Intake And Case Series Workflow

Use Case Intake when you do not yet have a final CRF but need to collect retrospective case material safely.

## What To Capture

- Case ID and short title.
- Typed note, dictated transcript, or OCR text from a photo/document.
- Evidence files such as case photos, audio notes, PDFs, discharge summaries, or screenshots.
- Age, sex, diagnosis, presentation, investigations, treatment, outcome, and follow-up.
- Missing items and de-identification warnings before analysis or publication.

The app stores the original text and evidence, then creates local structured suggestions. Treat these suggestions as a review aid, not a final clinical interpretation.

## Reporting Guidance Used

- CARE case report checklist: title, keywords, abstract, patient information, findings, timeline, diagnostics, intervention, follow-up, discussion, patient perspective, and consent.
- STROBE observational-study checklist: design, setting, participants, variables, data sources, bias, study size, analysis, results, limitations, and interpretation.
- JBI case series appraisal: inclusion criteria, standard measurement, consecutive/complete inclusion, demographics, clinical information, outcomes/follow-up, and statistical analysis.

References:

- https://www.care-statement.org/checklist

## Practical Retrospective Case Series Process

1. Add each case in Case Intake.
2. Upload evidence files and paste/dictate the clinical narrative.
3. Review the extracted group, diagnosis, treatment, and outcome.
4. Use Academic AI review only on de-identified material or when your study policy allows external AI.
5. Complete missing items before moving a case to ready status.
6. Convert stable repeated variables into formal CRFs when the case series becomes a larger study.
7. Export Case CSV for analysis.

## Privacy Rules

- Do not paste names, hospital numbers, phone numbers, addresses, or full dates of birth into external AI.
- Keep original evidence inside the local study database and encrypted backups.
- Publish only de-identified summaries.
- Keep consent/ethics decisions outside the app in your study records.
